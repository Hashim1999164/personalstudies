üåç Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions

What is it?

6 ways cultures differ ‚Üí affects how people work and behave in organizations

1. Power Distance

High: Boss is boss, don't question (China, Saudi Arabia)

Low: Everyone equal, can question boss (Sweden)

Management: High = Strict bosses, Low = Participative

2. Individualism vs Collectivism

Individualism: "I" first, personal goals (USA)

Collectivism: "We" first, group loyalty (Japan)

Reward: Person vs Team

3. Masculinity vs Femininity

Masculine: Competition, money, success (Japan)

Feminine: Care, cooperation, work-life balance (Norway)

Values: Achievement vs Quality of life

4. Uncertainty Avoidance

High: Many rules, fear change (Chile, Peru)

Low: Flexible, open to new ideas

Management: Rules vs Innovation

5. Time Orientation

Short-term: Traditions, quick results (Morocco)

Long-term: Future planning, patience (China, Japan)

Focus: Now vs Future

6. Indulgence vs Restraint

Indulgence: Enjoy life, freedom, fun

Restraint: Strict rules, controlled behavior

Culture: Flexible vs Disciplined

What is Planning?

Decide: What to do + How to do it + When to do it

Informal Planning

  • Not written
  • Short-term
  • Small places

Formal Planning

  • Written
  • Long-term
  • Big organizations

Why Plan?

Direction

Know where to go

Reduce Uncertainty

Less confusion

Save Money

No waste

Set Standards

Check later

Types of Goals

  • Financial Goals: Money, profit, cost
  • Strategic Goals: Beat competitors, grow market
  • Stated Goals: What company says
  • Real Goals: What actually happens

Types of Plans

Strategic Plans

Big picture, long-term, whole company

Operational Plans

Daily work, short-term, departments

Management by Objectives (MBO)

  • Boss + Employee set goals together
  • Check progress regularly
  • Reward based on results
  • Problem: Too much paperwork, not good for fast-changing work

üè¢ Organizational Structure

What is it?

How jobs are arranged in a company (who reports to whom)

Organizational Hierarchy

Bottom: Front-line employees

8 Key Elements

1. Work Specialization

Divide work into small tasks. Each person does one thing.

Good: More efficient | Bad: Too much = Boredom, stress

2. Departmentalization

Functional

By function (HR, Finance, Marketing)

Product

By product line

Geographical

By region

Customer

By customer type

3. Chain of Command

Clear line: Who reports to whom

4. Authority, Responsibility, Accountability

  • Authority: Right to give orders
  • Responsibility: Duty to do work
  • Accountability: Answerable for results
  • Unity of Command: Each employee has ONE boss only

5. Span of Control

Narrow Span

Few employees, close watch

Wide Span

Many employees, more freedom

6. Centralization vs Decentralization

Centralization

Top management decides everything

Decentralization

Lower levels also decide

7. Formalization

How many rules and procedures

High: Strict rules | Low: Flexible

Two Structure Types

Mechanistic (Rigid)

  • Rigid, centralized
  • Many rules
  • Narrow span
  • Good for: Stable environments

Organic (Flexible)

  • Flexible, decentralized
  • Few rules
  • Wide span
  • Good for: Dynamic environments

üëî Leadership

What is Leadership?

Ability to influence people to work toward goals

Management

Uses formal authority (position power)

Leadership

Uses influence and inspiration

Leadership Theories

Trait Theory (Leaders are BORN)

Extraversion

Confident, outgoing

Conscientiousness

Responsible, disciplined

Openness

Creative, flexible

Emotional Intelligence

Understand and manage emotions

Behavioral Theory (Leaders are MADE)

Leadership = Set of behaviors. Anyone can learn to be a leader.

Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) Theory

In-Group (Favorites)

  • Close relationship
  • More trust, rewards
  • More responsibility

Out-Group (Others)

  • Formal relationship
  • Less interaction
  • Fewer rewards

Charismatic Leadership

  • Strong personality
  • Inspire and motivate
  • Create emotional attachment

Transformational Leadership

  • Inspire followers to put company above self
  • Motivate change
  • Encourage innovation and growth

Trust and Leadership

Trust = Believe leader will act fairly and positively

How to build: Honesty, consistency, competence, loyalty, openness

‚úÖ Controlling

What is Controlling?

Monitor work ‚Üí Check if plans followed ‚Üí Fix mistakes

Simple: Make sure work is done as planned

Why Control?

Achieve Goals

Keep on Track

Give Feedback

Ensure Safety

Control Process (3 Steps)

Step 1: Measure Performance

How: Observation, reports, statistics

What: Satisfaction, budgets, output, sales

Step 2: Compare Performance

Compare actual results with standards

Check: Size of difference, direction, acceptable range

Step 3: Take Action

  • Do nothing (if difference is small)
  • Correct performance (immediate or basic)
  • Revise standards (if unrealistic)

Types of Control

Feedforward Control

Prevents problems BEFORE they happen

Concurrent Control

Controls work WHILE it's happening

Example: Direct supervision

Feedback Control

Takes place AFTER work is done

Helps improve future planning

Productivity

Productivity = Output √∑ Input

Output: Sales, services
Input: Labor, materials, costs
Higher productivity = Better efficiency

Balanced Scorecard

Measures 4 areas (all must be balanced):

1. Financial

2. Customer

3. Internal Processes

4. Learning & Growth

Direct Questions (Memorize These)

Q: Why is planning important?

A: Gives direction, reduces uncertainty, prevents waste, sets standards

Q: What are key elements of organizational structure?

A: Work specialization, departmentalization, chain of command, span of control, centralization/decentralization, formalization

Q: Leadership vs Management?

A: Management = Authority, Leadership = Influence (both needed)

Q: What is MBO?

A: Boss + Employee set goals together, review progress, reward based on results

Q: What is control process?

A: Measure performance ‚Üí Compare with standards ‚Üí Take corrective action

Scenario Questions (Understand These)

Scenario 1

Problem: Employees confused, don't know who to report to

Answer: Missing chain of command and unity of command

Scenario 2

Problem: Manager checks work while it's being done

Answer: Concurrent control

Scenario 3

Problem: Leader inspires with vision, encourages innovation

Answer: Transformational leadership

Scenario 4

Problem: Fast-changing environment, needs flexibility

Answer: Organic organizational structure

Scenario 5

Problem: Employees involved in decision-making

Answer: Decentralization and employee empowerment

Scenario 6

Problem: Two managers give instructions to same employee

Answer: Unity of command violated (should have one boss)

Scenario 7

Problem: Departments: HR, Finance, Marketing

Answer: Functional departmentalization

Scenario 8

Problem: Manager supervises too many employees

Answer: Span of control too wide
